Summer Scholarship Programme



Guidelines for Applicants

This section is intended to help applicants complete the form. All sections of the form must be fully completed. If you have any further questions on completing the form, please email the SSP Co-ordinator.

Please do NOT complete the on-line form in capital letters, as this makes it very difficult and unpleasant to read.

Personal Details

Please use this section to fill in the details of who you are and how we can contact you. If you have more than one first name, it would be helpful if you could use CAPITAL LETTERS to indicate the one which you prefer to use.

Current Course of Study

In this section you should enter details of your current course of study. This includes your main subject of study, and the department and institution where you are studying, the degree which you are working towards, e.g., BSc, BEng, MPhys, etc, and the stage of your course at which you currently are, e.g., year 3 out of 5. When giving grades for courses, it would be helpful if you could indicate what other gradings are given, or the maximum mark possible for the course.

Please note that only undergraduate students in their second year or above and students of taught MSc courses which will finish before the beginning of July are eligible for this Programme. Those already holding a degree who are studying for higher qualifications such as a research MSc or PhD are not eligible.

Qualifications

Please ONLY give details of examination results which count towards university entrance.

Computing Experience

As SSP projects always have a strong emphasis on computing, it is vital that applicants should be able to demonstrate that they are competent programmers. In particular, applicants should be familiar with the C and/or Fortran77/90 progamming languages. You should highlight any project work or course work which illustrates your ability to apply your computing skills to practical problems. We would also like to see some evidence that applicants can work as a member of a team - either in a formal project related to the course of study, or perhaps in other areas of life

Scholarships & Awards

In this section you should include details of any scholarships and/or awards which you have received during your academic career. We are looking for students who have strong academic backgrounds, so if you have received recognition for your abilities you should ensure that it is explained here.

Hobbies, Sports & Interests

In addition to being academically capable, we are also looking for applicants who have interests outside their work. We consider the social aspects of the SSP to be an important part of the programme and we look for applicants who would contribute towards it.

Person making Statement of Support

Please give details of the person who will be providing your Statement of Support. This should be your Director of Studies or equivalent.
NB: Applications cannot be accepted without an accompanying Statement of Support. It is up to you to ensure that we receive your Statement of Support before the closing date

Application Details

This section should focus on your reasons for making an application to the SSP, what you hope to gain from the programme, and what you have to offer. This is one of the most important parts of the form so you should spend some time on it.
