The Summer Scholarship Programme (SSP) is a unique training scheme; since its inception in 1987, more than 150 senior undergraduates have been employed to work on 10-week projects looking at various aspects of High Performance Computing.

The students are given a one week formal training course and then work on an assigned project with one, or sometimes two, supervisors. At the end of the period each project is written up in a formal report.

Electronic versions of past SSP reports are available for 1999, 1998, 1997, 1996, 1995, and 1994. There is also some information on the 1993 projects. During their stay the students are supported by grants from EPCC; competition for places is fierce with some 100 applicants for the available spaces.

At SuperComputing 1994 a past co-ordinator of the programme presented a paper describing in detail the operation of the programme. A copy of the paper is available here.
