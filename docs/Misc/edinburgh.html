Scotland: Page 1
  In the Land of Haggis and Fried Mars Bars  
  Ten weeks in Scotland  
 
  Robert Louis Stevenson, the famous Scottish writer, probably never said, "There are three things in Scotland: clouds, grass, and sheep." But he should have. On our trip to the Isle of Skye -- the second of our major journeys to the Highlands -- we ran into all three of these. Click on the picture for a closer view.  
 
  Queen's View is where thousands of travellers take this exact same picture every year. But we were fortunate enough to have a giant BBC helicopter repeatedly buzz within fifty meters of where we stood. Apparently, the BBC was diligently filming a documentary on the Highlands. Miraculously, this picture was taken without the helicopter in the way of our typical Scottish view.  
 
  Our team of explorers consisted of the 14 members of the Edinburgh Parallel Computing Centre's Summer Scholarship Programme, plus various people's girlfriends. Laboring over the map, (going clockwise from the left,) are Ute, Eurico, Giampetro, and Mario. Scott's leg has also made a cameo appearance on the far left.  
 
  Working at the EPCC. Like I said, there are fourteen of us from all over the world. We spend most of our time here, trying desparately to finish our projects for the program. Oddly enough, this picture with Krzysztof at the terminal is one of the two pictures ever taken in the lab.  
 
  Krzysztof and Marta... Aren't they cute? Marta eventually told us that she was making more money working in the heart of Edinburgh, than those of us slaving away in the flourescent-lit labs at the EPCC.
