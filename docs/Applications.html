Summer Scholarship Programme
Applications for the year 2000



The Summer Scholarship Programme is a unique training scheme; since its inception in 1987 more than 150 senior undergraduates have been employed to work on 10-week projects in the application of high performance computing to a wide variety of problems. In 1999 we took 11 students from France, Germany, Greece, the Republic of Ireland and the UK, working on projects ranging from modelling the impact of Genetically Modified crops on the flora associated with agriculture to developing a parallel benchmarking suite for Java Grande applications.

Students are given a one week formal training course and then work on an assigned project under supervision from a member of EPCC staff. At the end of the period each project is written up in a formal report. During their stay the students are supported by grants from EPCC; competition for places is stiff with some 150 applicants for the available places. EPCC will be offering 12 places in the year 2000. The Programme will begin in Edinburgh on Monday 3rd July 2000, and will run for 10 weeks. The scholarships are worth 150 pounds sterling per week. Where students are travelling from outside the UK we can arrange accommodation at a cost of around 60 pounds per week. Students have to pay their own costs travelling to and from Edinburgh.

Requirements

The Programme is open to talented senior undergraduates in any discipline with an emphasis on computer programming. Knowledge of C and/or Fortran is essential, but no experience of parallel computing is necessary. High academic marks are an advantage, but student selection is based primarily on programming ability and enthusiasm.

All teaching and supervision is English based. All applicants should be able to demonstrate a high level of fluency in English.

Application Procedure

Applications can be made in a number of ways:
  • On-line Web application form;
  • Standard form, PostScript version (GNUzipped);
  • Standard form, LaTeX version (tarred, GNUzipped; includes the Statement of Support);
  • Standard form, MS Word 7.0 version (zipped; includes the Statement of Support));
If you have problems applying with these forms, let us know and we'll send you a paper copy.

NB: In addition to the Application form, applicants should ask their Director of Studies (or equivalent) to fill in a Statement of Support. Applications will not be considered to be complete until we receive the Statement of Support, which should also be received by the deadline for applications.

The application deadline for scholarships in the year 2000 is 18 February 2000.

Please address any queries (by email) to epccssp@epcc.ed.ac.uk or (by fax) to +44 (0)131 650 6555, marked for the attention of the Summer Scholarship Co-ordinator.
