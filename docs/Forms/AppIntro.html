Summer Scholarship Programme



Guidelines for Applicants

This section is intended to help applicants complete the form. You should ensure that all sections of the form are fully completed. If you have any further questions on completing the form, please email the SSP Coordinator at the email address given above.

Personal Details

Please use this section to fill in the details of who you are and how we can contact you. If you have more than one first name, it would be helpful if you could underline the one which you prefer to use.

Current Course of Study

In this section you should add details of your current course of study. This includes your main subject of study, and the department and institution where you are studying, the degree which you are working towards, e.g., BSc, Beng, MPhys, etc, and what stage of your course you are currently at, e.g., year 3 out of 5. When giving grades for courses, it would be helpful if you could indicate what other gradings are given, or what a particular course is marked out of.

Qualifications

Please give details of exams which count towards university entrance only.

Computing Experience

As SSP projects always have a strong emphasis on computing it is vital that applicants should be able to demonstrate that they are competant programmers. In particular, applicants should be familiar with the C and/or Fortran77/90 progamming languages. You should highlight any project work or course work which illustrate your ability to apply your computing skills to practical problems.

Scholarships & Awards

In this section you should include details of any scholarships and/or awards which you have received during your academic career. We are looking for students who have strong academic backgrounds so if you have received recognition for your abilities you should ensure that it is explained here.

Hobbies, Sports & Interests

In addition to being academically capable, we are also looking for applicants who have interests outside their work. We consider the social aspects of the SSP to be an important part of the programme and we look for applicants who would contribute towards it.

Person making Statement of Support

Please give details of the person who will be providing your Statement of Support. This should be your director of studies, or equivalent.NB: Applications cannot be accepted without an accompanying Statement of Support. It is up to you to ensure that we receive your Statement of Support before the closing date

Application Details

This section should focus on your reasons for making an application to the SSP, what you hope to gain from the programme and what you have to offer. It is one of the most important parts of the form so you should spend some time on it.
